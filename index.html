Introduction

Auric Entertainment (Malaysia) is honoured to present "Hadagasma ‚Äì Bathiya & Santhush Unplugged Concert in Malaysia", featuring Sri Lanka's most iconic musical duo Bathiya & Santhush (BNS).

For over two decades, BNS has redefined Sri Lankan music by blending Sinhala, Tamil, English, and Hindi influences, becoming pioneers in the South Asian music industry. Their performances have mesmerized audiences worldwide, and they are celebrated for their ability to bring together diverse communities through music.

Historic Milestone: This will be the first full BNS concert ever held in Malaysia, exclusively for the Sri Lankan and South Asian community.

Beyond entertainment, this event carries a deeper purpose. Organized as a CSR initiative, 25% of net profits will be donated to the Maharagama Apeksha Hospital in Sri Lanka making this concert a cultural celebration with a lasting humanitarian impact.

Objectives of the Event

1. Strengthen Bilateral & Cultural Relations

  • To celebrate Sri Lanka's rich musical heritage in Malaysia and foster stronger cultural and diplomatic ties between the two nations.
  • To position Sri Lanka's modern music industry on an international platform, showcasing it to both Sri Lankans and the wider multicultural audience in Malaysia.

2. Unite the Sri Lankan Expatriate Community

  • To bring together Sri Lankans living in Malaysia and Singapore under one cultural roof.
  • To promote a sense of unity, identity, and pride within the expatriate community while keeping them connected to their homeland.

3. Support a Meaningful Cause (CSR Impact)

  • To donate concert profits to the Apeksha Hospital, Maharagama in Sri Lanka, directly contributing to cancer treatment facilities, research, and patient care.
  • To set an example of how Sri Lankan artists and communities abroad can collectively support the nation's healthcare system.

4. Promote Youth & Student Engagement

  • To encourage participation from the younger generation by offering a special student instalment ticket plan.
  • To inspire youth to engage in both cultural appreciation and social responsibility.

5. Establish Malaysia as a Cultural Hub for Sri Lankans

  • To create a landmark event that positions Malaysia as a regional hub for Sri Lankan cultural activities.
  • To lay the foundation for future cultural, musical, and CSR collaborations between Sri Lankans in Malaysia and beyond.

CSR & Social Impact

This concert is not just a musical celebration‚Äîit is a charitable initiative with long-lasting social value.

Direct Benefit to Sri Lanka

All net profits from the event will be donated to the Maharagama Apeksha Hospital, the country's leading national cancer treatment facility. This contribution will support patient care, advanced treatment facilities, and medical equipment.

Community-Driven CSR

By bringing together the Sri Lankan expatriate community in Malaysia and Singapore, the concert transforms entertainment into an opportunity for collective social responsibility. Every ticket purchased directly contributes to saving lives in Sri Lanka.

Raising Awareness

Beyond financial support, the event raises awareness within the diaspora about the importance of contributing back to national causes, encouraging solidarity and shared responsibility.

Cultural Diplomacy

The concert also stands as a bridge of goodwill, showcasing how Sri Lankan culture and music can serve as a platform for humanitarian impact, strengthening both Sri Lanka's image abroad and its bonds with Malaysia.

In essence, this project goes beyond music‚Äîit combines culture, compassion, and community, leaving a lasting legacy for both Sri Lankans abroad and those in need at home.
